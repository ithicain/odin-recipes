Chantarelle Powder

Description

If you are interested in foraging your own food and live in a region where Chantarelles are plentiful, consider powdering your finds to preserve it as a delicious spice. In fact, powdered mushrooms make an excellent replacement for MSG due to its potent umami flavor.

Use a tablespoon or two of your end product to add to recipes such as soup, noodle dishes, eggs, and just about anything you can think of.

Ingredients/Tools

  • 5 lbs chantarelle mushrooms
  • Oven that can reach 150 degrees
  • Spice or coffee grinder
  • Tupperware or glass container with lid

Steps

  1. Harvest and clean your mushrooms. Try your best to brush off dirt, rather than rinsing so the mushrooms do not soak up too much water.
  2. Cut your mushrooms into 1/4 inch slices and arrange them on parchment paper on a baking sheet.
  3. Make sure none of your mushrooms are touching each other!
  4. Place into oven at 150 degrees for 60 minutes. Flip mushrooms, then put back into the oven for an additional 60 minutes.
  5. Your mushrooms will be done if they are much smaller and crispy.
  6. Place mushrooms in grinder and produce a fine powder. If you find that the powder still contains moisture, it's OK to place powder back into oven for additional 20 minutes before grinding again.
